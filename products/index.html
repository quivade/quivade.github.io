Building For the Future

Quivade was spun out of Warsaw University of Technology, best technical school in Poland. Before starting the company our founders took part in projects funded by The National Centre for Research and Development and Ministry of National Defence Republic of Poland.

 

Current Contributors

Jakub Kopański

Until Quivade Jakub was managing group of engineers developing Bluettoh Low Energy radio PHY at Warsaw Univarsity of Technology. That work was part of project ordered by Ministry of National Defence. He was also involved in creating SoC for biomedical applications, where he was responsible for ECG/EMG signal acquisition. Before joining the university Jakub held engineering position at Evatronix (now part of Cadence) where he developed high speed serial link IPs.

Krzysztof Siwiec

Krzysztof is a Reaserch Assistant at Warsaw University of Technology. He is the original author of Quivade phase correction method. Nowdays he is leading a design team developing a SoC for bio-signal acquisition and processing. His long time expertise was recognized when he won Europractice Design Contest in 2010. This competition was sponsored by UMC who donated free tapeout for Krzysztof's winning design.

Paweł Narczyk

Pawel is pursuing PhD at Wasaw University of Technology. He also works at TechInsights as circuit analyst. At the university he is engaged in SoC development for biomedical application, where his focus is on high precision circuitry insusceptible to process, temperature and supply voltage variations.
